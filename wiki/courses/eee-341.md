# EEE 341 — Engineering Electromagnetics

**School:** Arizona State University, Spring 2026
**Instructor:** Cemil Geyik (lectures by Prof. James T. Aberle)
**Schedule:** Tuesday/Thursday 6:00pm–7:15pm, SCOB 250
**Credits:** 4

## Overview
Time-varying electromagnetics built on top of EEE 241 review: starts from Maxwell's equations and free-charge dynamics, develops plane-wave propagation in lossy/lossless media, treats reflection and refraction at boundaries, then transmission lines (Smith chart), waveguides + cavity resonators, and finally antennas + radiated waves. Heavy lab component (5 labs) using ADS / HFSS-style simulators.

## Roadmap (Canvas modules, in order)

### Module 1 — Foundations of Electromagnetism
- Review of electrostatics + steady current
- Review of magnetostatics + Faraday's law
- Free charge dissipation in a conductor
- [[displacement-current]]
- [[boundary-conditions-em]]
- ==[[maxwell-equations]] in time-varying form==
- Charge–current continuity, EM potentials

### Module 2 — Electromagnetic Plane Waves
- Unbounded plane waves + simple medium
- Time-harmonic fields, [[complex-permittivity]], [[helmholtz-equation]]
- [[plane-wave-lossless]] propagation
- [[plane-wave-lossy]] propagation
- Special cases: low-loss dielectric, good conductor, skin depth
- Current flow in a good conductor (surface impedance)
- [[wave-polarization]] (linear / circular / elliptical)
- Power flow and the [[poynting-vector]]

### Module 3 — Reflection and Transmission of Plane Waves
- Normal incidence: [[fresnel-coefficients]] $\Gamma, \tau$
- Normal incidence on a PEC (standing waves)
- [[snells-law]] of reflection + refraction
- Oblique incidence — perpendicular polarization
- Oblique incidence — parallel polarization
- [[total-internal-reflection]] (critical angle, evanescent waves)
- [[brewster-angle]] (zero reflection for parallel polarization)
- Reflectivity and transmissivity (power)

### Module 4 — Transmission Lines
- [[transmission-line-model]] — distributed R, L, G, C
- Telegrapher's equations, characteristic impedance $Z_0$
- [[reflection-coefficient-line]] $\Gamma_L$, VSWR, line impedance
- [[smith-chart]] — purpose, key features
- Impedance matching: quarter-wave transformer, lumped-element, single-stub
- Transients on transmission lines (bounce diagrams)

### Module 5 — Waveguides and Cavity Resonators
- Fiber optics
- Hollow tube waveguides
- General relations for $\vec{E}$ and $\vec{H}$ in waveguides
- [[waveguide-modes]] — TEM, TE, TM
- TM and TE modes in rectangular waveguide
- [[waveguide-cutoff]] — cutoff frequencies and propagation
- Phase and group velocities, dispersion ($\omega$–$\beta$) diagrams
- [[cavity-resonator]] — resonant modes, Q factor

### Module 6 — Antennas and Radiated Waves
- Antenna properties overview
- [[hertzian-dipole]] (the elemental radiator)
- [[antenna-gain-directivity]] — gain, directivity, radiation resistance, beamwidth
- [[half-wave-dipole]] — practical wire antenna
- Effective area of a receiving antenna
- [[friis-formula]] — link budget
- Aperture antennas, antenna arrays
