# David MacKay

**Affiliation:** Professor of Natural Philosophy, Cambridge University (deceased 2016).
**Author of:** *Information Theory, Inference, and Learning Algorithms* (Cambridge, 2003) — free at https://www.inference.org.uk/mackay/itila/book.html. See [[textbook-mackay-itila]].

## Why he matters to Jayden

Two reasons:

1. **LDPC and belief propagation.** MacKay rediscovered and popularized LDPC codes in the 1990s — single-handedly responsible for their use in 5G, DVB-S2, Wi-Fi. His treatment in ITILA Ch 47–50 is the clearest in print. **The identity "LDPC decoder = belief propagation = GNN inference"** is the link from classical coding to modern neural decoders ([[belief-propagation]], Nachmani 2018).

2. **Intuitive information theory.** ITILA is famously readable. Shannon entropy, mutual information, channel capacity, all explained from first principles with working examples.

Later in life, MacKay turned to energy policy (*Sustainable Energy — Without the Hot Air*, 2008), a separate but widely-read book.
