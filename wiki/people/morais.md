# João Morais

**Current:** Wireless Software Engineer, NVIDIA
**Previous:** PhD, ASU Wireless Intelligence Lab under Ahmed Alkhateeb (graduated 2025)

## Why he matters to Jayden

**The single most strategic "preparatory contact" in the roadmap.** Morais made exactly the trajectory — Wi-Lab → NVIDIA — that Jayden wants to emulate. The roadmap explicitly recommends emailing him, with a light-touch question, early (Phase 3 M8 or M9).

From the roadmap:
> "Practice [cold emails] by sending two preparatory emails earlier — to João Morais at NVIDIA about his path from Wi-Lab, and to Umut Demirhan (DeepMIMO maintainer) with a dataset-related question after your Phase 3 project. Both are former Wi-Lab students who remember what it was like to be you."

## Research themes

1. **Wireless dataset similarity and comparison** — Morais et al. 2026 "Wireless Dataset Similarity" (arxiv:2601.01023); "Comparing Stochastic and Ray-tracing Datasets" (arxiv:2512.12449, Asilomar 2025). These are the best papers for understanding when a ray-traced training set generalizes to a measurement set.
2. **V2V communication with DeepSense** — **Morais, Charan, Srinivas, Alkhateeb 2024** "DeepSense-V2V" (arxiv:2406.17908, IEEE TVT 2025). V2V scenario in the DeepSense dataset.
3. **Ongoing work at NVIDIA** — unspecified publicly; likely Sionna / AODT integration.

## What to email him for

- **Not** about a PhD application (he's not the decision-maker).
- **Not** about internships (he's not a recruiter).
- **Yes:** a short, specific question prompted by one of his papers — e.g., "I read your Dataset Similarity paper and am trying to apply the metric to [specific case]; do you have advice on [specific decision]?"
- **Yes:** a very short career question — "could you share what the transition from Wi-Lab to NVIDIA was like?" — people are almost always happy to answer.

Keep it under 150 words. Follow up at most once.

## Why reaching out pays

He was Jayden's exact profile 5 years ago. He remembers. Former students almost always respond to current students if the message is specific and low-effort. A reply from him is also a **signal** Jayden can reference later — "I talked to João Morais about his Wi-Lab experience" is credible in a Wi-Lab cold email and does not name-drop inappropriately.
