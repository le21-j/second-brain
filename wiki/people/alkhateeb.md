## Why he matters to Jayden

**Primary PhD target.** Wi-Lab is one of the two labs the [[python-ml-wireless]] is built around. Alkhateeb explicitly invites cold emails ("We are always looking for highly-motivated Ph.D. students"). Strong letters + a DeepMIMO/DeepSense/Sionna project portfolio is the admission pathway.

## Research themes (2024–2026, in order of activity)

1. **Foundation models for wireless** — [[large-wireless-model]] (arxiv:2411.08872), LWM-Spectro (arxiv:2601.08780), LWM-Temporal (arxiv:2603.10024). All on Hugging Face at `wi-lab/lwm*`.
2. **Digital twins for 6G** — the vision paper arxiv:2301.11283. Multiple build-out papers 2023–2026. See [[wireless-digital-twin]].
3. **Multi-modal sensing-aided beamforming** — [[deepsense-6g]]. Beam prediction, vision + LiDAR + radar fusion.
4. **RIS + O-RAN + ISAC** — the arxiv:2510.20088 MILCOM 2025 Best Demo paper.
5. **Generative AI for wireless** — Wireless Dataset Similarity (arxiv:2601.01023), Generative Decompression (arxiv:2602.03505).

## How to approach

Per the roadmap's Phase 5 guidance: **send a focused cold email between late May and early September 2027**. 150–200 words. Structure:
1. One line: junior at ASU, GPA, EE concentration.
2. One sentence: specific recent Alkhateeb paper + a substantive technical observation (not a compliment).
3. 2–3 bullets: concrete artifacts + GitHub links (LWM reproduction, DeepSense Challenge rank, DeepMIMO contribution).
4. Clear ask: applying for Fall 2028 PhD; is he accepting; would summer engagement be useful.

Do **not** send until at least one LWM/DeepMIMO/Sionna repo is live. Follow up exactly once, 2–3 weeks later.
