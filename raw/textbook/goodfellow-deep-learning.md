# Deep Learning — Goodfellow, Bengio, Courville

**Category:** Deep Learning (theoretical reference, the "old testament")
**Status:** FREE (HTML)
**URL:** https://www.deeplearningbook.org/
**Authors:** Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio, Aaron Courville
**Publisher:** MIT Press, 2016
**Roadmap phase:** reference, esp. Ch 5–10, 14–15

## Topic coverage
- Part I (math refreshers): linear algebra, probability, numerical computation, ML basics.
- Part II: Deep feedforward networks, regularization, optimization, CNNs, sequence modeling, practical methodology, applications.
- Part III: Linear factor models, autoencoders, representation learning, structured probabilistic models, Monte Carlo, partition functions, approximate inference, deep generative models.

## Why it's on the roadmap
Historically the first comprehensive DL textbook; dated on current practice (pre-transformer) but **still the clearest exposition of the theoretical foundations**. Specifically:
- Ch 5 (ML basics) — formal bias-variance, generalization, VC.
- Ch 6–7 (feedforward + regularization) — still the canonical references.
- Ch 8 (optimization for DL) — SGD/Adam analyses.
- Ch 9 (CNNs) — theoretical motivation.
- Ch 10 (RNNs, LSTMs) — historical but careful.
- Ch 14–15 (autoencoders, representation learning) — precursor to modern self-supervised.

## What's dated
- No coverage of transformers (2017).
- No coverage of diffusion models.
- Chapter on generative models (GANs) is the original treatment, now superseded by Prince.
