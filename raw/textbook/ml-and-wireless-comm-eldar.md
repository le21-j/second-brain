# Machine Learning and Wireless Communications — Eldar, Goldsmith, Gündüz, Poor (eds.)

**Category:** Wireless / ML (current academic reference)
**Status:** paid book — not in repo
**Publisher:** Cambridge University Press, 2022
**Editors:** Yonina Eldar (Weizmann), Andrea Goldsmith (Princeton), Deniz Gündüz (Imperial), H. Vincent Poor (Princeton)
**Roadmap phase:** reference throughout Phase 3–4

## Topic coverage (representative chapters)
- Fundamental ML tools for comms (supervised, unsupervised, deep learning)
- ML for modulation classification and signal detection
- Deep learning for channel estimation and decoding
- Federated and distributed learning over wireless channels
- Task-oriented (semantic) communications
- Physical-layer security via ML
- Resource allocation with deep reinforcement learning
- Integrated sensing and communication (ISAC)
- Edge intelligence, IoT, 6G architectures

## Why it's on the roadmap
Single most authoritative 2022 edited volume in the ML+wireless space; editors are four of the most senior names in wireless + signal processing. For a junior researcher, this is a reference, not a linear read — use it as the **adult version of Simeone's monograph** when you need rigor.

## What to do with it
Read chapter intros + the two or three chapters closest to your current project (channel estimation, federated learning, E2E autoencoder) to see the canonical formulation before diving into individual papers.
