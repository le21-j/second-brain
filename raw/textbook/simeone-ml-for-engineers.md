# A Brief Introduction to Machine Learning for Engineers — Osvaldo Simeone

**Category:** Machine Learning (engineer-oriented primer)
**Status:** monograph — free preprint on arXiv (2018)
**arXiv:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1802.05374
**Publisher:** Foundations and Trends in Signal Processing, 2018
**Author:** Osvaldo Simeone (King's College London)
**Roadmap phase:** Month 1 complement

## Topic coverage
- Review of probability, linear algebra, convex optimization (for engineers, not CS students)
- Supervised learning: linear regression/classification, SVMs, kernel methods, bias/variance
- Unsupervised learning: PCA, GMMs, EM
- Deep learning: MLPs, CNNs, autoencoders
- Probabilistic graphical models (Bayesian networks, Markov random fields)
- The signal-processing-ML dictionary (Karhunen-Loève = PCA; Kalman = linear-Gaussian SSM)

## Why it's on the roadmap
The roadmap calls this "the best engineer-oriented ML primer." Written by a communications theorist for signal-processing readers — so it explicitly leans into the analogies that make ML painless for DSP people (regularized LS = ridge; Kalman = state-space; belief propagation = sum-product). This is the fastest way for a DSP-trained undergrad to get to the parts of ML that matter for wireless.

## Why this matters for Jayden specifically
Your [[eee-350]] probability background — Bayesian inference, MLE, confidence intervals, stochastic processes — is already most of the classical-ML stats chapter. Simeone explicitly bridges the gap. Read it as a 3-day sprint, not a semester-long text.
